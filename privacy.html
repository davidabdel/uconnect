2. Information we collect

We collect personal information that you provide to us directly and information that is generated automatically when you use our services.

Information you provide

  • Contact details — your name, phone number, email address and any message content you submit through our enquiry forms.

  • Usage and device data — IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited and referring URLs.

3. How we use your information

We use your personal information to:

  • Respond to your enquiries and provide the services or information you request;

  • Operate, maintain and improve our websites, products and services;
  • Verify your identity and prevent fraud, spam and abuse;
  • Comply with our legal, regulatory and record-keeping obligations.

SMS / Text Message Communications

The information below describes how we handle mobile phone numbers and consent for SMS/text messaging, consistent with the requirements of Twilio, mobile carriers and applicable messaging regulations.

4. SMS & mobile messaging

When you provide your mobile phone number and opt in to receive SMS/text messages from us, the following applies:

  • Consent: We send SMS messages only to individuals who have expressly opted in, for example by submitting a form with your mobile number and agreeing to be contacted, or by messaging us first. Consent to receive SMS is not a condition of purchasing any goods or services.
  • Types of messages: Messages may include responses to your enquiries, appointment or service updates, account or transaction notifications, and — where you have opted in to marketing — promotional offers and news about our brands.
  • Message frequency: Message frequency varies based on your interaction with us. You will not receive an excessive number of messages.
  • Cost: Message and data rates may apply, depending on your mobile carrier and plan.
  • Opt out (STOP): You can cancel SMS messages at any time by replying STOP to any message. After you send STOP, we will send one confirmation message and will not send any further SMS messages unless you opt in again. You may also contact us at david@uconnect.com.au to be removed.
  • Help (HELP): For help, reply HELP to any message or email us at david@uconnect.com.au.
  • Carrier liability: Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages.

No sharing of mobile opt-in data

Mobile information (including your phone number and SMS consent) will not be shared with or sold to any third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. Information may be shared only with the sub-processors and service providers strictly necessary to deliver the messaging service you have requested (for example, our SMS platform provider). All other categories of data-sharing exclusions in this policy continue to apply. Text messaging originator opt-in data and consent are never shared with any third parties for their own marketing.

5. How we share your information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share it only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: Trusted third parties who perform services on our behalf (such as hosting, CRM, SMS/messaging delivery and analytics), bound by confidentiality and data-protection obligations, and only to the extent needed to provide the service.
  • Within the group: Between UConnect and the brands we own and operate, to service your enquiry.
  • Legal requirements: Where required by law, regulation, legal process or enforceable government request, or to protect our rights, safety and property.
  • Business transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition or sale of assets, subject to the protections in this policy.

As stated above, mobile phone numbers collected for SMS and the associated consent are never shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing purposes.

6. Data retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this policy, to comply with our legal and regulatory obligations, resolve disputes and enforce our agreements. When information is no longer required, we securely delete or de-identify it.

7. Data security

We implement reasonable technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal information against unauthorised access, loss, misuse or alteration. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Your rights and choices

Subject to applicable law, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you;
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • Request deletion of your information;
  • Withdraw consent to marketing communications, including SMS (reply STOP) and email (use the unsubscribe link);
  • Make a complaint about how we handle your information.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at david@uconnect.com.au. Australian users may also contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at www.oaic.gov.au.

9. Cookies

Our websites use cookies and similar technologies to function correctly, remember your preferences and analyse traffic. You can control cookies through your browser settings; disabling some cookies may affect site functionality.

10. Third-party links

Our sites and messages may link to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to review their privacy policies.

11. Children's privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can delete it.
